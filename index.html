Risk 360 Platform

Fundamental problems with management of operational risks brings following factors into the play. viz. framing, cognitive bias and groupthink. Contrary to other risks operational risks are usually not willingly incurred nor are they revenue driven. Also, operational risk is not diversifiable i.e. if people, systems, and processes remain imperfect, operational risk cannot be fully eliminated.

The solution is targeted at the loan account maintenance team which can take preventive measures if the asset quality indicator drops beyond certain level. Risk underwriting team will also be able to use the system for ad hoc credit assessment. The system provides dashboard for the users based on the role and automated periodic alerts and reports. Portfolio level dashboard would be available for top management. The dashboard would allow drill-down based on asset categories, geography and other parameters.

The proposed solution leverages analytics to reduce the information overload on the team and provide them with actionable insights. The platform would collect information from publicly available sources.

The platform will help the banks manage risk from three sides

  Reducing the Probability of Default by creating early warning list

  Reducing Loss Given Default (LGD) by maximizing collateralization for sectors or customers on early warning list

  Reducing Exposure at Default (EAD) by early interventions, mandatory actions and reducing exposure to high-risk geographies, industrial clusters

Proposed solution will have two variants SCREEN360 for Due-diligence, Screening and Assessment and EWS360 for Ongoing-monitoring.

 

SCREEN360

The purpose of this variant is to avoid risk knowledge gap with better due diligence. A superficial evaluation is not sufficient to proactively assess and mitigate risk. This variant will focus on screening clients and customers against PEPs, 100+ sanction, OFAC, AML/CFT and defaulter list, and internal lists as part of KYC process. This will be especially useful for Trade Finance desk which handles Letter of Credit / Bank Guarantees from overseas entities.

This variant will vet potential customers beyond financial stress scores to capture an accurate picture of risk. This information may include:

  Financial, legal, and business information on private, public, and international entities

  Company profiles and information beyond typical self-reported data

  Negative news, employee, and customer views

  Compliance and litigation history

 

EWS360

Currently, the banks monitoring is done through information submitted by the customer in the form of stock statement, monthly select operational data (MSOD), quarterly information system (QIS), half yearly operating statement and credit audit report. The credit audit is conducted at two places one at branch level, another at central level. Monitoring in the form of payment to suppliers and receipt from customers is also done by the operations team. However, external events like accident / fire at factory, change in management, death of the promoter/partner, labour strike, customer and employee discontent, unfavorable industrial policy changes etc. are not monitored.

This variant will uncover hidden risks using open-web information sourced from news, business, legal, and social media. Early-warning tracking of small changes in customers situations can make a big impact on the bottom line of banks. Better credit monitoring can be one of the main ways that capital-strapped banks can improve their business model. This product variant monitors open-web sources for early warning indicators and effectively reduces net exposure at default. Armed with always up-to-date perspective and automated alerts, banks will get real-time insights and reduce vulnerability.

Solution Expectations

The purpose of the solution is to create efficient monitoring mechanism is automated, streamlined, and consistent across the asset-class. The solution will provide required drill down of the information so that the source can be tracked, if needed thereby creating audit trail of the generated report.

 

Core Triggers and

Early Warning System

  Integration with internal Technical Triggers like overdrawing, delayed payment

  Manual user triggers based on operational data

  External data triggers

  Segregating accounts into different watchlist categories based on pre-defined risk buckets

Management of Watchlist

  Movement from one bucket to another

  Recoding of Actions and List of Recommended Actions for user

  Reporting of actions taken and control-return for success/failure of action

Back-testing

  Ability to back-test and categorise the accounts based on historical crawled records (say, two years)

 

The solution should have a workflow management system which will address following automations and reporting

  Flag ratio - % of customers flagged by the system and transferred to watch list

  Exceptions How many of the restructuring or SMA (Special Mention Accounts) are not there in the watchlist.

  Selectivity How many substandard accounts as opposed to regular accounts are flagged by the system

  Time What is the average time of default after system triggers an alert

 

 

Following items should be taken into consideration while designing the system

  The watch-list should have several categories to be managed based on different-buckets of risk severity.

  Tracking of mitigating actions taken and recording of the same.

  Knowledge management to ensure that lessons learned from successful mitigation are automatically applied to new watch-list assets.
